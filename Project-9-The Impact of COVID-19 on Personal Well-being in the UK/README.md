# Project 9: The Impact of COVID-19 on Personal Well-being in the UK

## Introduction

The COVID-19 pandemic affected many aspects of daily life, including mental health and overall well-being. Understanding how personal well-being changed during and after the pandemic can provide valuable insights into the broader public health impact of major societal disruptions.

This project compared average personal well-being scores before and during the COVID/Post-COVID period using official UK Office for National Statistics (ONS) data.

## Data Source

Office for National Statistics (ONS).

Personal Well-being Estimates, UK: April 2011 to March 2023.

Available at:
https://www.ons.gov.uk/peoplepopulationandcommunity/wellbeing/datasets/headlineestimatesofpersonalwellbeing

This project uses official UK national statistics rather than a simulated dataset.

## Research Question

How did personal well-being change during and after the COVID-19 period compared with pre-COVID levels?

## Methods

* Descriptive analysis
* Period comparison (Pre-COVID vs COVID/Post-COVID)
* Data transformation using R
* Data visualization using ggplot2

## Key Findings

* Life satisfaction decreased by approximately 0.14 points.
* Feelings that life is worthwhile decreased by approximately 0.07 points.
* Happiness decreased by approximately 0.06 points.
* Anxiety increased by approximately 0.27 points.

The largest change was observed for anxiety, suggesting that psychological well-being was particularly affected during and after the pandemic.

## Public Health Relevance

Population well-being is an important indicator of societal health. Monitoring changes in well-being during major public health events can help identify areas requiring support and inform future public health planning and policy responses.
