# Project 10: Self-Harm Rates by Sexual Orientation in England and Wales

## Introduction

Intentional self-harm is an important public health concern and a recognised indicator of psychological distress. Understanding which population groups experience the greatest burden can help inform targeted prevention and support strategies.

In this project, I analysed official Office for National Statistics (ONS) data to explore differences in age-standardised self-harm rates by sexual orientation and sex in England and Wales between 2021 and 2023.

## Data Source

Office for National Statistics (ONS)

Self-harm by sexual orientation, England and Wales, 2021–2023.

## Research Question

How do intentional self-harm rates vary by sexual orientation and sex in England and Wales?

## Methods

* Descriptive analysis
* Comparison of age-standardised rates
* Visualisation using grouped bar charts with 95% confidence intervals
* Public health interpretation

## Key Findings

* Self-harm rates were substantially higher among LGB+ populations compared with heterosexual populations.
* Among females, the self-harm rate for LGB+ individuals was nearly three times that of heterosexual females.
* Females consistently experienced higher rates than males across all sexual orientation groups.
* The highest rates were observed among Gay/Lesbian and Bisexual females.

## Public Health Relevance

The findings highlight important mental health inequalities within the population. Identifying groups experiencing a disproportionate burden of self-harm can help inform targeted interventions, prevention strategies, and mental health support services.
